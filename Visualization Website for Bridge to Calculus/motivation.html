Motivation

Our client in this project is Bridge to Calculus (BTC), a non-profit organization in Boston that focuses on improving the math scores of BPS students and helping them achieve a better academic future. They were founded after many courageous people saw the discrepancy in Boston's demographics and took action. Our project focuses on visualizing how the math skills of students in BPS have evolved over time so that BTC can revisit the data, similar to how Professor Raj did all the way back in 2007. In her presentation, she used charts which became influential in the way BTC grew; we designed our visualizations hoping we could have a similar effect. While standardized tests certainly have their flaws, they are understandbly used to benchmark and track progress for students. For this reason, BTC asked us to analyze data for the two main standardized tests BPS students take: National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) and Massachusetts Comprehensive Assessment System (MCAS). Find more about these tests at this bottom of this page!

Here is a sample of some of the motivating questions we discussed with the client and generated ourselves:

  • Whites and Asians typically lead the charts, are Blacks and Hispanics catching up or lagging behind?
  • 4th and 8th grade are important years for testing, are the demographic discrepancies widening or narrowing as students get older?
  • Are the demographic trends reflected in Boston also reflected at the state-level? Are they worse or better?
